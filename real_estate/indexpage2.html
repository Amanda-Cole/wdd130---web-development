Ready to buy a house?

So you are ready to buy a house! What an exciting time in your life and I am here to help you every step of the way. Here are some steps that we need to take before we can really get going.

Meet with Mortgage lender and get preapproved

Meeting with a Mortgage Lender is the first step in buying a home either for yourself, or as an investment. Plan to have paystubs, tax returns, and other valid financial documents ready. Do Not make any major purchases or financial changes during this time. Once you have spoken to your Lender, and know what loan you are approved for and comfortable with then we set a budget and start the process of looking for your new home.

Confirm your Agent

I will ask you to sign a contract that states you have chosen me to be your Agent, and for what time period. This is a step that protects me and you as we start the adventure of finding you a home. We will preview listings everyday, and then we will start going in person. Contract will be filled out and down payment collected. Once the seller signs then we are under Contract. Then comes inspections and appraisals.

Thinking of an Investment Property?

Cash is king when it comes to investment properties. If you don’t have enough cash to purchase an investment property that’s ok! Speak to a lender and they will help you understand what you can qualify for. Leave your standards at home. Understanding that an investment property is all about making profit, and not about buying a home that you would personally live in is essential.

Flipping vs. Renting

Flipping a house means that you purchase it, remodel it, and then sell it for a profit. This can be a great way to put your money to work but there are risks. But watch the clock as time is money when flipping a house Buying a home and renting it out is a great long-term investment. Rental rates have gone up allowing investors an opportunity to make an additional monthly income.

Emergency Money

Investment properties have a risk like all investments do. I strongly encourage investors to keep 6 months of their property expenses on hand in case the renter can’t pay the rent or improvements need to be made. Property Management companies list your rental, interview the candidates, do the background checks, show the property, handle the lease, collect the rent, handle repair requests, and carryout the eviction process if necessary. Check there fees and see which one is right for you.
